Chole Bhature Recipe

Delicious and spicy Punjabi dish with fluffy fried bread and tangy chickpea curry.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (kabuli chana), soaked overnight
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with salt until soft.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Add onions, ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Saute until golden brown.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until they soften. Add spices and cooked chickpeas. Cook for 10-15 minutes.
  4. For Bhature, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, yogurt, sugar, baking powder, and salt. Knead into a soft dough.
  5. Divide dough into small balls and roll out into circles. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown.
  6. Serve hot Chole with Bhature, garnished with fresh coriander leaves.
Dal Makhani Recipe

Creamy and flavorful lentil dish cooked with butter and cream, perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (sabut urad dal)
  • 1/4 cup red kidney beans (rajma)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak black lentils and kidney beans overnight. Pressure cook until soft.
  2. In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Add onions, ginger, garlic, and green chilies. Saute until golden brown.
  3. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates. Add spices and cooked lentils.
  4. Simmer on low heat for 20-30 minutes. Add cream and garnish with fresh coriander leaves before serving.
Dosa Recipe

Traditional South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, served with chutney and sambar.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds separately for 6 hours.
  2. Grind soaked rice and dal separately into a smooth batter. Mix both batters, add salt, and ferment overnight.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladleful of batter, spread into a circle, and drizzle oil.
  4. Cook until golden brown on both sides. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.
Idli Sambhar Recipe

Classic South Indian breakfast dish consisting of steamed rice cakes (idli) served with a flavorful lentil-based curry (sambhar).

Ingredients:

  • For Idli:
  • 1 cup idli rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram lentils)
  • Salt to taste
  • For Sambhar:
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 carrot, chopped
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 1/2 cup mixed vegetables (beans, peas, etc.)
  • 2 tablespoons sambhar powder
  • 1 teaspoon tamarind paste
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Soak idli rice and urad dal separately for 4-6 hours. Grind into a smooth batter, mix, and ferment overnight.
  2. Steam idlis in idli molds until done.
  3. For Sambhar, pressure cook toor dal until soft. In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, onions, and tomatoes. Cook until soft.
  4. Add mixed vegetables, sambhar powder, tamarind paste, and cooked dal. Simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and garnish with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with idlis.
Kanda Poha Recipe

Popular Maharashtrian breakfast dish made with flattened rice, onions, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups flattened rice (poha)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 potato, diced
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 cup peanuts
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Rinse poha in water and drain. Keep aside.
  2. In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, onions, and green chilies. Saute until onions turn translucent.
  3. Add diced potatoes, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until potatoes are soft.
  4. Add rinsed poha and mix well. Cook for a few minutes until heated through.
  5. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
Upma Recipe

Traditional South Indian dish made from semolina, vegetables, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • 1/4 cup green peas
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Dry roast semolina until lightly golden. Keep aside.
  2. In a pan, heat oil, add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and asafoetida. Let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions, green chilies, and grated ginger. Saute until onions turn translucent.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes, grated carrot, and green peas. Cook until vegetables are slightly soft.
  5. Add roasted semolina and mix well. Add salt and water (2 cups for 1 cup of semolina).
  6. Cook covered on low heat until the upma is cooked and water is absorbed.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
Khaman Dhokla Recipe

Popular Gujarati steamed savory cake made from gram flour (besan).

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1/4 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-green chili paste
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon fruit salt (eno)
  • Salt to taste
  • For Tempering:
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • A few curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, mix gram flour, yogurt, water, oil, ginger-green chili paste, sugar, and salt to make a smooth batter.
  2. Grease a steaming plate and pour the batter into it. Steam for 15-20 minutes until cooked through.
  3. Once cooked, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Cut into pieces and prepare the tempering. Heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, curry leaves, green chilies, and asafoetida. Let them splutter.
  5. Pour the tempering over the dhokla pieces. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  6. Serve warm with green chutney.
Fafda Jalebi Recipe

Ingredients for Fafda:

  • 2 cups besan (gram flour)
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 teaspoon carom seeds (ajwain)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Ingredients for Jalebi:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tablespoons cornflour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
  • A pinch of orange food color (optional)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. For Fafda: Mix besan, turmeric powder, asafoetida, carom seeds, and salt in a bowl. Add water gradually to make a smooth dough. Rest the dough for 15-20 minutes. Roll out thin strips of dough and fry until golden brown.
  2. For Jalebi: Mix all-purpose flour, cornflour, baking powder, and orange food color (if using). Add water gradually to make a smooth batter. Rest the batter for 15-20 minutes. Fill it in a piping bag.
  3. For the sugar syrup, heat sugar and water in a pan until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add saffron strands.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Squeeze the batter in a circular motion into the hot oil to form jalebis. Fry until they turn crisp and golden.
  5. Dip the fried jalebis in the warm sugar syrup for a few seconds and then remove.
  6. Serve the crispy Fafda with sweet, syrupy Jalebis.
Khandvi Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • For Tempering:
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon sesame seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit
  • Few curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves

Instructions:

  1. Mix gram flour, yogurt, water, turmeric powder, and salt in a bowl to form a smooth batter.
  2. Pour the batter into a non-stick pan and cook on low heat, stirring continuously until it thickens.
  3. Quickly spread a thin layer of the batter onto a greased surface before it sets. Let it cool slightly.
  4. Once cooled, cut the spread into thin strips and roll them tightly.
  5. Arrange the rolled Khandvi pieces on a plate.
  6. For tempering, heat oil in a pan, add mustard seeds, sesame seeds, asafoetida, green chilies, and curry leaves. Pour this tempering over the arranged Khandvi pieces.
  7. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  8. Serve Khandvi with green chutney or sweet chutney.
Shahi Paneer Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 200g paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/4 cup cashew nuts, soaked in water
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/4 cup cream
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Heat ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and saut until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates.
  5. Meanwhile, grind the soaked cashew nuts into a smooth paste.
  6. Add cashew paste to the pan and mix well.
  7. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add paneer cubes and mix gently.
  9. Add cream and garam masala. Simmer for 2-3 minutes.
  10. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve hot with naan or rice.
Rajma Chawal Recipe

Ingredients for Rajma:

  • 1 cup rajma (red kidney beans), soaked overnight
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Ingredients for Chawal (Rice):
  • 1 cup basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 cups water
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook soaked rajma with salt until soft and cooked.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut until golden brown.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add tomato puree, green chilies, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  6. Add cooked rajma along with the water it was cooked in.
  7. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the flavors are well combined.
  8. For rice, boil water in a pan. Add soaked rice and salt. Cook until the rice is done.
  9. Serve hot rajma with steamed rice.
Tadka Dal Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup pigeon peas (toor dal), washed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Instructions:

  1. Pressure cook washed toor dal with salt until soft and mushy.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds. Let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and saut until translucent.
  4. Add ginger-garlic paste and saut until the raw smell disappears.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  6. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  7. Add cooked toor dal and mix well. Adjust consistency by adding water if needed.
  8. Simmer for 5-10 minutes. Add garam masala and mix.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with rice or roti.
